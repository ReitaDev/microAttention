# Why I built this

There's a version of this story where I say I built microAttention to deepen 
my technical understanding, to bridge theory and practice, to complement my 
research. All of that is true and none of it is the real answer.

The real answer is that I wanted to prove I could actually build and code. Not 
as a performance for anyone else as proof to myself (and bragging rights). I've spent a long time 
thinking and writing about these systems, and thinking and writing well, but 
there's a particular kind of confidence and satisfaction that only comes from making something 
work. From having the thing run and output something real and knowing you built 
every part of it.

I'm also genuinely fascinated by how these systems behave. How a slightly 
different number changes the outcome completely. How sensitive the whole thing 
is underneath the surface that most people interact with. I wanted to 
experiment with that, to not only read and theorise  about it, experimenting and building allows me to
watch it happen in front of me.

And there's something else, in my opinion If I'm going to 
write in the language of LLMs,  and make arguments about what 
these systems can and cannot hold, whose knowledge they centre, what gets lost 
in the compression, then it only makes sense to understand them from the 
inside. being able to look at it beyond the architecture as a concept but the mechanism as something 
I've touched. Built in components, piece by piece, until the thing that felt 
abstract becomes something I actually know.

Because one day I want to do more than describe these systems or audit them. 
I want to design something that reflects what I actually believe and seek. And 
you can't design what you don't understand from the inside out.

This is the inside out part.
